II. Gender Imprisonment A. Punishment Sex Ratio 2. Military Service

Effects of War and Peace on Punishment Sex Ratios


Sex ratios of publicly authorized punishment have varied widely across history. Much evidence indicates that criminal justice systems have deeply entrenched gender animus against men. During wartime, military service has substituted for criminal punishment in disposing of men. Substitution between military service and punishment suggests that public bias toward sacrificing men’s lives isn’t just a response to military emergencies. Sacrificing men in military service and in punishment are alternate outcomes of the same discursive process. Anti-men gender bias in public discourse is pervasive and largely ignored.
