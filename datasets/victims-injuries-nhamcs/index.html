Leading Causes of Injuries in U.S. by Sex Since 1992 (NHAMCS)


Leading causes of injury-related visits to hospital emergency departments in U.S. by sex in 1992, 1992 through 1995, and 2001, according to NHAMCS.

Get dataset in form/version:
  • Google Sheet published to web (fast, resilient)
  • Microsoft Excel workbook (primary version)
  • Apache OpenOffice Calc spreadsheet
  • Google Sheet (downloadable)
  • Microsoft Excel workbook (secondary, use only if above fail)

Dataset sheets:

  • 1992 injury causes: leading causes of injury-related visits to hospital emergency departments in 1992 by sex for persons ages 14 to 44, 18 and over, and all ages, with injury sex ratio; based on National Hospital Ambulatory Medical Care Survey (NHAMCS) data
  • 1992-95 injury causes: similar data for 1992-95
  • 2001 injury causes: similar data for 2001
  • illness & injury: injury and illness by sex and age groups in 1992-95, based on NHAMCS
  • processing notes: data processing steps to replicate NHAMCS injury tabulations
  • cause categories: codes for broader grouping of injury causes in ICD-9-CM

Related datasets:

  • serious injuries from domestic violence, intimate-partner violence & other violence
  • claims dataset: domestic violence is the largest cause of injury to women
  • gender disparity in services for victims of domestic violence
