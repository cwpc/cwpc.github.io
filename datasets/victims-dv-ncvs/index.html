II. Gender Imprisonment C. Domestic Violence 1. Criminal Suspicion

Intimate-Partner Violence by Sex: NCVS vs. NEISS


Comparison of NCVS and NEISS estimates of intimate-partner violence by sex, U.S. about 2010, with analysis of NCVS-defined “serious” domestic violence and simple-assault shares in domestic violence by sex.

Get dataset in form/version:
  • Google Sheet published to web (fast, resilient)
  • Microsoft Excel workbook (primary version)
  • Apache OpenOffice Calc spreadsheet
  • Google Sheet (downloadable)
  • Microsoft Excel workbook (secondary, use only if above fail)

Dataset sheets:

  • intimate-partner violence: NEISS hospital visits due to intimate-partner violence compared to NCVS reported intimate-partner violent victimizations, by sex, U.S. 2008
  • criminal victimization: share of “serious” domestic violence and intimate partner violence, as reported in U.S. Bureau of Justice Statistics publication, Criminal Victimization 2011 (based on NCVS), with comparison to domestic violence other than simple assaults, by sex

Related datasets:

  • physical injuries from violence and criminalization of violence, by sex
  • claims dataset: domestic violence is the largest cause of injury to women
  • homicides, suicides, occupational fatalities by sex and cause
